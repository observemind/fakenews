About SatyaCheck

A deep dive into India's fake news crisis and how AI can help fight it.

The Problem: India's Misinformation Crisis

India is one of the world's largest consumers of social media, with over 500 million WhatsApp users. This scale, combined with low media literacy in some demographics, creates a fertile ground for misinformation to spread.

From election-time propaganda to health misinformation during COVID-19, fake news has caused real-world harm — including mob violence, communal tensions, and public health crises.

Our Solution

SatyaCheck is an AI-powered fake news detection system trained specifically on Indian news patterns. It combines classical Machine Learning with modern NLP to deliver fast, accurate verdicts on news content.

The system uses TF-IDF vectorisation with n-gram analysis (1–3 grams) to capture contextual patterns, followed by a Logistic Regression classifier trained on India-specific real and fake news corpora.

Future Roadmap

SatyaCheck v2 will include:

  • BERT / IndicBERT multilingual model for Hindi, Tamil, Telugu
  • Real-time fact-checking via external knowledge bases
  • WhatsApp API integration for automated checking
  • Source credibility scoring alongside content analysis
  • Browser extension for inline news fact-checking
  • Image and video deepfake detection module

Built With Open Source

SatyaCheck stands on the shoulders of giants. We use Python, scikit-learn, NLTK, Flask, and Chart.js — all open-source tools that power the global AI community.

This project is designed as a contribution to India's growing AI-for-social-good ecosystem.
